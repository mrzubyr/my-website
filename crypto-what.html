What is Cryptocurrency?

Learn how digital currencies work and why they matter.

Cryptocurrency is digital or virtual money that uses cryptography for security, making it almost impossible to counterfeit or double-spend. Unlike traditional currencies issued by governments (like Naira or USD), cryptocurrencies are decentralized and usually operate on a blockchain, which is a public ledger of all transactions.

Popular cryptocurrencies include Bitcoin (BTC), Ethereum (ETH), and USDT. People use crypto for investment, trading, remittances, and sometimes for buying goods and services online.

Key features of cryptocurrency:

  • Decentralized – no central authority controls it
  • Secure – uses cryptography for transactions
  • Transparent – transactions are recorded on a blockchain
  • Pseudonymous – you can transact without revealing personal info
